Trailer Type & Weight Speed Limit Built-Up Speed Limit Single Carriageway Speed Limit Dual/Motorway Special Requirements
Small Trailer (<750kg) 30 mph 60 mph 70 mph Treated as car extension; no speed reduction
Standard Caravan (750kg-1500kg) 30 mph 50 mph 60 mph Must have working brakes; proper coupling; breakaway cable

Plant/Equipment Trailer 30 mph 40-50 mph 50-60 mph Depends on trailer weight; professional requirements apply
Towing Important Notes:
  • Maximum towing capacity depends on vehicle - check manufacturer specs
  • Driver must have appropriate towing experience and qualifications
  • Trailers over 750kg must have functioning brakes
  • Double white lines cannot be crossed when towing
  • Additional mirrors required when towing (at least 4.25m tall when coupled)
  • You cannot tow more than one trailer at a time in the UK

Key Points to Remember

✓ Speed Limit Facts:
  • Speed limits are MAXIMUM speeds, not targets - reduce for conditions
  • The National Speed Limit sign (white oval with diagonal line) indicates you can drive at the maximum for that road type
  • Always obey posted signs - they override national limits
  • In poor weather, fog, ice, rain - reduce speed regardless of posted limit
  • Speeding fines range from £100 to £1,000+ depending on severity
  • Speed cameras monitor compliance on UK roads
  • HGVs are fitted with speed limiters (56 mph for rigid, 100 km/h for articulated)
  • Towing significantly reduces maximum safe speed - always use lower limits
✓ Road Safety Tips:
  • Always maintain a safe distance - use 2-second rule minimum
  • In heavy traffic with contraflow, stay alert and don't change lanes
  • Give extra space to large vehicles and motorcycles
  • Watch for vulnerable road users: pedestrians, cyclists, elderly
  • Be extra cautious at night, in rain, ice, or fog
  • Use appropriate dipped headlights in poor visibility
  • Check your vehicle regularly - especially brakes and tires
  • Never exceed speed limit regardless of traffic conditions
  • When towing, plan longer journeys with breaks for vehicle cooling
  • Report hazards to highway authorities if safe to do so
